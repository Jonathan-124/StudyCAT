Let's start with an example. If Anna has 2 cookies and Bob has 4 cookies, how many cookies do they have in total?
It's not too difficult to figure out that they have 6 cookies in total. But let's take a step back and think about how we got the answer. We first counted the 2 cookies that Anna had. Then, we counted 4 more to get the total.

In short, we have performed addition! When we have two groups of things (cookies in this case), and want to know the total amount, we add the two numbers together.

To rephrase what we did in the example more simply, we could say '2 plus 4 ', where plus is another way to say 'add'. Because writing 'plus' becomes tiring after a while, we use the symbol \( + \) (called a plus sign) instead to write it as $$ 2+4 $$ Since the result of \( 2+4 \) is equal to 6, we can also write $$ 2+4=6 $$ Let's go through some exercises.

Exercise 1

Fill in the blank. $$ 3+5= \square$$


Show solution
The correct answer is \( 3+5=8 \).

We first count to 3. Then, we count 5 more from 3, and get 8. Because the result of \( 3+5 \) is the same as the number 8, we write \( 3+5=8 \).


Exercise 2

Charlie has five hats and Dylan has two hats. How many hats do they have in total?


Show solution
The correct answer is 7.

First, let's write the numbers using digits. Charlie has 5 hats, and Dylan has 2. Because we are counting the total number of hats, we need to add the two numbers \( 5+2 \). Finally, the result of \( 5+2 \) is 7, which is the number of hats they have in total.


To summarize addition:
  • Addition calculates the total of two numbers.
  • Addition is represented by the plus sign (\( + \)).
