Recall that we use comparison signs to compare numbers. We have already learned the three most common comparison signs used: the greater-than sign (\(>\)), the lesser-than sign (\(<\)), and the equals sign (\(=\)). In addition to these three signs, there are three more comparison signs that are less commonly seen, but nonetheless useful in certain situations.

The first sign is the "greater-than or equal to sign", denoted by \(\geq\). This sign is used when the number on the left is greater than or equal to the number on the right. For example, we can say \( 6 \ge 2 \), which means "6 is greater than or equal to 2".

The second sign is the "less-than or equal to sign", denoted by \(\leq\). This sign is used when the number on the left is less than or equal to the number on the right. For example, we can say \( 3 \le 7 \), which means "3 is greater than or equal to 7".

The third sign is the "not equal to sign", denoted by \( \not= \). This sign is used when the number on the left is not equal to the number on the right. For example, we can say \( 3 \not = 7 \), which means "3 is not equal to 7".

Here is a summary of all comparison signs we have learned so far:
Sign Name of Sign Usage Example Example in words
\(>\) Greater-than sign \(6 > 2 \) "Six is greater than two"
\(\geq\) Greater-than or equal to sign \(6 \geq 2 \) "Six is greater than or equal to two"
\(<\) Less-than sign \(3 < 7 \) "Three is less than seven"
\(<\) Less-than or equal to sign \(3 < 7 \) "Three is less than or equal to seven"
\(=\) Equals sign \(5 = 5 \) "Five is equal to five"
\(\not =\) Not equal to sign \(6 \not = 5 \) "Six is notequal to five"


Exercise 1

Select all choices that can fill in the blank: $$ 1 \ \square \ 3 $$


Show solution
The correct answers are \(<\), \(\leq\), and \(\not =\).

1 is less than 3, so both \(<\) and \(\leq\) can fill in the blank. Since 1 is not equal to 3, \(\not =\) can also fill in the blank.


Exercise 2

Select all choices that can fill in the blank: $$ 4 \ \square \ 4 $$


Show solution
The correct answers are \(\leq\), \(\geq\), and \(=\).

Since 4 is equal to 4, \(=\) can go in the blank. \(\leq\) and \(\geq\) are also true, since both signs are allowed when the two numbers we are comparing are equal.
