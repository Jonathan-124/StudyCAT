Up until now we have dealt with numbers that are greater than 0, which are also called positive numbers. Numbers like \(1\), \(27\), and \(1000000\) are all examples of positive numbers. Negative numbers are all numbers that are smaller than 0. Negative numbers are still numbers, and use the same digits as the positive numbers we encountered so far. The only difference is, negative numbers have a negative sign (\(-\), also known as a minus sign) in front to indicate that it is a negative number. Numbers like \(-1\), \(-27\), and \(-1000000\) are all examples of negative numbers.

We encounter negative numbers a lot in our daily lives. For example, when the temperature outside is cold, we might have a weather report of it being \(-2\) degrees outside. When we spend more money than we have in a bank account, the account statement might show a statement with \(-10\). In some large buildings with basements, an elevator buttons might have a \(-2\) label. These are all examples of negative numbers, and they all indicate numbers that are less than 0.

Exercise 3

Order \(-12\), \(1\), \(-54\), \(0\) from least to greatest.


Show solution
The correct ordering is \(-54\), \(-12\), \(0\), \(1\).

\(-54\) and \(-12\) are negative numbers, and are therefore to the left of \(0\) on the number line. \(-54\) is to the left of \(-12\) on the number line, therefore \(54 < 12\). \(-54 < -12 < 0 < 1\) is the four numbers ordered from least to greatest.
