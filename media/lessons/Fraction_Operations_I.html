Just like how we can multiply and divide whole numbers, we can also multiply and divide fractions with each other.

To multiply a fraction with another fraction, we simply multiply their numerators together to get the resulting fraction's numerator, and multiply their denominators together to get the resulting fraction's denominator. For example, if we want to multiply \(\frac{2}{3}\) and \(\frac{2}{5}\), we multiply them like so \(\frac{2 \times 2}{3 \times 5}\) to get the result of \(\frac{4}{15}\).

Exercise 1

Calculate \(\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{5}{6}\) and give your answer as a simplified fraction.


Show solution
The correct answer is \(\frac{5}{9}\).

\(\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{5}{6} = \frac{2 \times 5}{3 \times 6} = \frac{10}{18}\). \(\frac{10}{18}\) is not a simplified fraction, as 10 and 16 have a common factor of 2. \(\frac{10}{18} = \frac{10 \div 2}{18 \div 2} = \frac{5}{9}\), therefore \(\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{5}{6} = \frac{10}{18}= \frac{5}{9}\).

We often multiply by a fraction when we want to scale a number by the fraction. For example, if I wanted to know what is one half of \(\frac{3}{4}\), we get the answer by multiplying the fraction \(\frac{3}{4}\) by \(\frac{1}{2}\) to get \(\frac{3}{8}\).

Exercise 2

\(\frac{2}{3}\) of students in a school take the bus home. Of the students who take the bus home, a third of them live in the downtown area. What fraction of students take the bus downtown to go home?


Show solution
The correct answer is \(\frac{2}{9}\).

\(\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{1}{3} = \frac{2}{9}\).

To divide a fraction and another fraction, we first invert the divisor fraction by switching its numerator and denominator, then multiply the dividend by this new inverted fraction. For example, if we want calculate \(\frac{2}{3} \div \frac{1}{5}\), we first invert the divisor \(\frac{1}{5}\) to get a new fraction \(\frac{5}{1}\). Then, we multiply the dividend with the new fraction like so \(\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{5}{1} = \frac{2 \times 5}{3 \times 1}\) to get the result of \(\frac{6}{5}\).

Exercise 3

Calculate \(\frac{5}{2} \div \frac{3}{4}\) and give your answer as a simplified fraction.


Show solution
The correct answer is \(\frac{10}{3}\).

\(\frac{5}{2} \div \frac{3}{4} = \frac{5}{2} \times \frac{4}{3} = \frac{5 \times 4}{2 \times 3} = \frac{20}{6}\). \(\frac{20}{6}\) is not a simplified fraction, as 20 and 6 have a common factor of 2. \(\frac{20}{6} = \frac{20 \div 2}{6 \div 2} = \frac{10}{3}\), therefore \(\frac{5}{2} \div \frac{3}{4} = \frac{20}{6}= \frac{10}{3}\).


Exercise 4

Calculate \(\frac{3}{2} \times 3\) and give your answer as a simplified fraction.


Show solution
The correct answer is \(\frac{9}{2}\).

Before we multiply, we must first convert the number 3 into a fraction. \(\frac{3}{2} \times 3 = \frac{3}{2} \times \frac{3}{1} = \frac{3 \times 3}{2 \times 1} = \frac{9}{2}\). Since \(\frac{9}{2}\) is already a simplified fraction, the answer is \(\frac{3}{2} \times 3 = \frac{9}{2}\).
