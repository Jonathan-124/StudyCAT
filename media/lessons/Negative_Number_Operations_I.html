We can add and subtract negative numbers the same way we add and subtract whole numbers.

Let's look at adding negative numbers first. Recall that when we add two numbers, we start at the first number, then go to the right the distance of the second number to get the sum.

For example, when we calculate \(3+5\), we start from 3 and then take 5 steps to the right to get the sum of 8.



With negative numbers, the process is exactly the same. For example, when we calculate \(-3+5\), we start from -3 and then take 5 steps to the right to get the sum of 2.


Here's another example. When we calculate \(4+\scriptsize - \normalsize 5\), we start from 4 and then take -5 steps to the right to get the sum of -1. Because the second number is a negative number, we go in the opposite direction of the direction we were supposed to move. In other words, even though we go to the right when adding, because we are adding a negative number we go left, the opposite direction.

Show solution
\(-7+1=-6\)

Starting from -7, we take 1 step to the right to get the sum of -6.

We also subtract negative numbers the same way we subtract whole numbers. Recall that when we subtract two numbers, we start at the first number, then go to the left the distance of the second number to get the difference.

For example, when we calculate \(10-2\), we start from 10 and then take 2 steps to the left to get the difference of 8.

With negative numbers, the process is exactly the same. For example, when we calculate \(-2-3\), we start from -2 and then take 3 steps to the left to get the difference of -5.


Here's another example. When we calculate \(-3- \scriptsize - \normalsize 5\), we start from -3 and then take -5 steps to the left to get the difference of 2. Because the second number is a negative number, we go in the opposite direction of the direction we were supposed to move. In other words, even though we go to the left when subtracting, because we are subtracting a negative number we go right, the opposite direction.
