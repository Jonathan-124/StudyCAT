I am a student. How should I use this website?

Whether you are currently enrolled in a course (e.g. AP calculus AB), studying for a test (e.g. SAT Math I subject test), or just want to learn some math for fun, update your profile with the course you want to study. If you have indicated a test date while selecting a course, recommendations on your study pace will be present. When you click on the course in the courses menu the first time, take a placement test to see how much of the course you've completed, and what topics you still need work on. In each unit, the personalized topic progression graph should indicate to you which lessons to take next. Exit tests after each lesson are indications of how well you have grasped the knowledge from each lesson, and the algorithm records your personal knowledge growth. Best of luck in your studies!


I am a teacher. How should I use this website?

The website is well suited for formative assessment. Note that because most answer evaluations are done client-side, the answering process is not secure and therefore should not be used for summative assessment. Classroom integration is a future goal that, when implemented, can be used by teachers to see how well their students are grasping material corresponding to learning objectives.


I have spotted an error/a bug/a typo. How can I contact you?

First of all, sorry! As the developer I write and format all lessons and questions by myself, and often make mistakes. Because there are so many lessons/questions/answers on this website, I probably will not notice this error unless a user points it out. If you could kindly submit your feedback via the "Report a Bug" button at the bottom of most pages, I would try my best to fix it ASAP.


How can I contribute?

If you are a user, you can help just by using the site! Data on response patterns is very useful for calibration, and your bug reports and written feedback always help improve the site. If you have expertise in computerized adaptive testing or item response theory, please contact me directly as I would immensely appreciate your input.


More details on the algorithm?

Concepts/learning topics are arranged in a hierarchical skill tree, where fundamental skills must be learned before more complicated ones. These dependencies are modelled using directed graphs, where each node is a concept and directed connections between nodes represent their relationships. Each exit test tests the user's proficiency at a certain node, and the user's "knowledge graph" is updated depending on their score on the exit test.


Technical details of the website?

The Django and Django REST frameworks along with PostgreSQL are what make the backbone of the website backend. Bootstrap, jQuery, the Guppy math editor, and the CytoscapeJS and KaTeX libraries are the cornerstones of the website frontend.
